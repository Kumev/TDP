PC OR CONSOLE GAMING?

Posted on 7 years ago by Evans Kumah

Which is better in gaming?

You’re probably here because you’re thinking about getting a gaming system but you’re not really sure whether to get a personal computer or a console later in the future or you’re thinking about transitioning from either a personal computer to a console or a console to a personal computer but you are not too sure about which is better for gaming. I’ll be very happy to help you in that decision by comparing video game consoles and Personal Computers where we will go into depth the hardware, software, the pros, and the cons.

Trust me, after reading this article you would have made your mind about what to get.

Choosing between a PC and a Console

Choosing between a PC and a console depends on the conditions of everyone. Not everyone is in a condition where he or she can afford to pay for electricity whenever his or her excessive power consuming gaming system uses up all of the electricity. Besides, everyone has their tastes and preferences. This article will help you choose which system will be best for you. Without wasting more of your time, let’s get this match started.

Consoles

The main producers of video game consoles are Sony, Microsoft, and Nintendo. The latest consoles from these three companies are PlayStation 4 Pro from Sony, Xbox One X All Digital Edition from Microsoft and Nintendo Switch from Nintendo. The most prominent of these video game consoles is the PlayStation 4 with total sales of 94.70 million units. Its main competitor is the Xbox One made total sales of 42.20 million units. The Nintendo Switch sold 32.94 million units. These consoles are purposely made for gaming but have other functions that enable users to watch videos, stream TV series and movies, stream music, browse photos and the internet.

Pros:
  • One good thing, probably the best is that there is no need to install the game on the console since it comes in physical discs.
  • Compared to personal computers in some cases it is cheaper.
  • Console Exclusive Games
Cons:
  • It is difficult and uncommon to upgrade its hardware components since its parts may not be compatible with future technology.
  • It has a smaller multiplayer base.
  • Games for consoles are brand specified. i.e. You cannot play an Xbox game on a PlayStation.
  • They limit the functionality of the game.
  • They can only be used for gaming, listening to music, watching videos and exploring photos.

Personal computers

Personal computers are a different thing altogether. When it comes to choosing a personal computer for gaming you have to know what you want. You have to know if you are going for a gaming laptop or a desktop and if you are going for a desktop are you purchasing the system or building it up on your own. When it comes to gaming a good personal computer will give you almost perfect gaming experience. Well, personal computers are widely preferred by professional gamers to consoles. Building a high-end desktop PC yourself is quite expensive but worth it. In this case, you get to handpick all the components before it is assembled. You get to pick the motherboard, CPU, GPU (Graphics Card), RAM and the hard disk…. What else could one ask for?

Pros:
  • A PC can be used for a very long time with regular upgrades.
  • It provides an awesome gaming experience as long as the hardware is very good.
  • It has a large game library and is compatible with almost every game, including the ones released a century ago.
  • PCs allow the user to accomplish a lot more tasks other than playing games.
  • It has a big multiplayer base.
Cons:
  • The game must be installed after purchasing it.
  • It is quite expensive to maintain.
  • It consumes a lot of power.

Which is best for gaming?

If you are a person who isn’t too much worried about the quality and gaming experience, doesn’t have much to spend on building your system and paying for electricity or/and is not looking forward to gaming professionally then I suggest you go for the console. Let us say you have decided to play games professionally then I would recommend PC gaming, to be specific, building your PC. Although building your PC requires quite a large sum of cash it’s worth the sacrifice if you’re looking forward to your gaming career being successful since you get to choose everything to your satisfaction and capabilities. In this case, you know the in and out of your PC and you will be able to attend to it yourself when there are minor problems.

Which is best for you?

If you are looking for the best gaming experience go ahead and build your personal computer. If you are like me and you prefer building the PC on your own, I mean who doesn’t want a machine fitted with 64 GB RAM, multiple GPU, and a mind-blowing SSD. When it comes to consoles you don’t have to worry about the specifications of the hardware in the console since every game released by developers during its time of recognition will be made in such a way that it can be run on the console.

At this point, you should know what you have to get. If it is a PC, your choices will be between a laptop and a desktop PC. I suggest you go for the desktop PC and if you go down that path you’ll to choose between building yours or purchasing one from a shop. If you need some advice when it comes to building your PC you can read this article. If it is a console and you are having a hard time selecting which console to get read this article. I prefer the PlayStation but everyone has their preference so the article will help you find yours.

 I hope this article was beneficial most of you have been able to come to a final decision. If you have any questions feel free to send an email or ask it in the comments section. Do not be reluctant to reach out if you have any suggestions. Thank You for your time and have a great day.
