Is the Xbox Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller Worth it?

Posted on 7 years ago by Evans Kumah

Gaming becomes an even broader topic to discuss every year. The upcoming release date of Google’s cloud gaming service Google Stadia even makes it more interesting. The competition gets tougher as more innovative products keep on creating a buzz. The Xbox Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller has been released and announced. Microsoft released the Xbox Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller on November 4, 2019, for $179.99. This is almost $30 more than the Xbox One Elite Wireless Controller. The original Elite Wireless Controller had quite a lot of problems concerning its grips that peel off, failed bumpers, and the textured grips slipping out of place. It now sells on Amazon for $99. The Xbox One Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller comes with newer features, more accessories and fewer problems.

What’s new?

The new controller is lighter than its original, comes with adjustable tension thumbsticks, USB-C port, Bluetooth connectivity and wraparound rubber grips that cover a large area. The adjustable tension thumb sticks will allow the sensitivity of the thumb stuck to be changed. This can be done by simply lifting off the thumbstick and using the key to switch between three levels. You can switch between high tension, medium tension and low tension and replacing the thumbstick after the adjustment is done. The USB Type C port will allow the charge way faster than the original series.

The case has a flap at the area the charger goes through allowing you to charge the controller while it’s still in the case. Since the controller has Bluetooth connectivity users will be able to connect the controller to their Windows 10 PCs.

If you got your hands on the elite controller you can recall it had two different profiles you can switch between but the new Xbox One Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller has a third profile which some users may find convenient but just so you know you can still switch back to the default controller settings. The hair-trigger lock now also has three settings that you can switch based on your game style. Users can switch the triggers from fully opened to medium to closed. The Series 2 controller now has inbuilt rechargeable batteries which are a bother to most people since they prefer just switching the batteries and getting on with the game than having to wait for the controller to charge to enjoy their wireless gaming but since a USB-C charger will be used the controller will charge faster than you think it would.

Accessories

There are a variety of accessories in the case of the controller. Users can switch between the style of thumbsticks they prefer to use, they can now choose from the standard, classic and wide dome thumbsticks. You can also swap the d-pad choosing either the standard d-pad or the faceted d-pad. The product also comes with a USB type C charger cable and only one tool stick.

Controller (the original elite controller) which had a lot of complains after a few months of use mostly about the grip falling off. $30 may not be that huge of a difference since it has new settings and profiles and the Bluetooth addition. Hopefully, Microsoft worked on its quality control issues on its new controller

My Final Thoughts

The Xbox One Elite Series 2 Wireless Controller may not be worth a $150 but it is worth getting it. I think you won’t be running into any problems many people faced with the Xbox One Wireless Controller. Failing bumpers and problematic grips feeling of are not that difficult to fix for a brand like Microsoft. But to be safe and if you are not in a hurry to get the controller it will be best to wait a few months to be aware of any problems the controller may have before making a final choice.
Thank You for your time and have a good day.
