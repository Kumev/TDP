Google Stadia game lineup nearly doubles days before launch day

Posted on 7 years ago by Evans Kumah

Like most people you have been awaiting the launch of Google Stadia and now that it’s only a few days away you are more excited than ever. As exciting as this is for us all, the games available on launch day may bring your hopes down. Not because they are bad games but because it’s not a large list of titles.

The game lineup before the addition was a 12 title list of interesting must-have games but it turns out Google closed some last-minute deals with some publishing companies just in time of the launch day, allowing Google to add 10 more games to the lineup for launch day.

Game lineup for Google Stadia launch day

Newly Added
  • Attack on Titan: Final Battle 2
  • Farming Simulator 2019
  • Final Fantasy XV
  • Football Manager 2020
  • Grid 2019
  • NBA 2K20
  • Rage 2
  • Metro Exodus
  • Trials Rising
  • Wolfenstein: Youngblood
Older games
  • Assassin’s Creed Odyssey
  • Destiny 2: The Collection (available in Stadia Pro)
  • Gylt
  • Just Dance 2020
  • Kine
  • Mortal Kombat 11
  • Rise of the Tomb Raider
  • Red Dead Redemption 2
  • Samurai Shodown (available in Stadia Pro)
  • Shadow of the Tomb Raider
  • Thumper
  • Tomb Raider 2013

These new additions make the highly anticipated launch more exciting. The other games in the list of games will be available sometime in 2020. 22 games still seem to be a small number when compared to Microsoft’s xCloud service. xCloud has 50 titles in early preview. Among these 22 games are some interesting but old titles. Old in the sense that they have been released for a while and no one will be that excited to play them compared to a newer title like Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order. It has been reported that this service may still have some problems, but hopefully these problems are or will be fixed, luckily you don’t even have to install patches.

Just like Destiny 2: The Collection, Samurai Shodown will be made available to Google Stadia Pro users for free.
